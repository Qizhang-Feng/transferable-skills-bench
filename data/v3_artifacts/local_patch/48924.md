# Solution Note: Listing Missing Dates from a Sequence

The data was located in **Sheet1**. Column A contained a sequence of dates starting from row 2 (with "Date" as the header in row 1). The task required identifying all missing dates from the continuous sequence and placing them into column C starting at cell **C2**.

## Approach

The solution used Python with openpyxl to:

1. **Read the date sequence** from column A (cells A2 onwards) in Sheet1, extracting all existing dates and converting them from datetime objects to date objects for comparison.

2. **Determine the date range** by finding the minimum and maximum dates from column A to establish the full expected sequence.

3. **Identify missing dates** by generating a complete date range from the minimum to maximum date and comparing it against the existing dates in column A. Any dates present in the complete range but absent from column A were considered missing.

4. **Write the missing dates** to column C starting at cell C2 (C2:C13), formatting them as datetime objects to match Excel's date format.

The logic iterated through each day in the expected range, checked for its presence in the original dataset, and collected all gaps. These missing dates were then written sequentially to the target range in column C.
