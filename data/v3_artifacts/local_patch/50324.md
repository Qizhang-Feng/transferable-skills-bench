# Cross-Selling Percentage Analysis Solution Note

The task calculated the percentage of customers who purchased two products together from the 'Datensatz' sheet and populated the results in the 'Analyse' sheet.

**Data Source (Datensatz sheet):**
- Customer names were in column C, rows 4-33 (30 customers total)
- Product headers were in row 3, columns D-K (Products 1-8)
- Purchase data (sales values) were in the range D4:K33
- A non-zero value indicated a customer purchased that product

**Output Location (Analyse sheet):**
- Product labels were in row 4, columns D-K (horizontal axis)
- Product labels were in column C, rows 5-12 (vertical axis)
- Formula results were written to range D5:K12

**Logic Applied:**
For each cell in the output matrix, the formula counted how many customers purchased BOTH products (row product AND column product) by checking if both corresponding cells in 'Datensatz' were non-zero. This count was divided by the total number of customers who purchased the row product, then multiplied by 100 to get a percentage. The COUNTIFS function was used to count customers with non-zero values for both products, while COUNTIF counted customers for the denominator. Results were formatted to 9 decimal places using the ROUND function with precision parameter 9.
