# Solution Note: Rolling Price Increase Calculator

The task involved calculating updated customer rates with sequential price increases applied over time based on effective dates and frequency intervals.

**Data Structure:**
- Sheet name: `Data`
- Header row: Row 13
- Data rows: 14-42
- Historical baseline rates: Columns L through W (12 months of historical data)
- Effective start date: Column X
- Frequency (months between increases): Column Y
- Price increase percentages: Columns Z, AA, AB, AC (up to 4 sequential increases)
- Output columns: AD through AO (12 columns for calculated rates)

**Python/openpyxl Approach:**

The solution read the baseline rates from columns L-W for each customer row (14-42). For each output month in columns AD-AO, the logic determined:
1. The number of months elapsed since the effective start date (column X)
2. Which price increase wave applied based on the frequency value (column Y)
3. The cumulative percentage increase by multiplying applicable increases from columns Z, AA, AB, and AC

The formula applied the baseline rate from the corresponding historical month (columns L-W) and multiplied it by the cumulative increase factor (1 + sum of applicable increases). Each cell in the output range AD14:AO42 contained a formula that dynamically calculated the adjusted rate based on the customer's specific parameters and the time period represented by that column.
