# Solution Note: Month-over-Month Headcount with Terminations

The task required fixing a SUMPRODUCT formula to correctly calculate monthly headcount by excluding terminated employees from the count after their termination date.

## Spreadsheet Structure
- **Sheet Name**: Sheet1
- **Header Row**: Row 1
- **Data Start Row**: Row 2
- **Hire Date Column**: Column A (A2:A22)
- **Term Date Column**: Column B (B2:B22)
- **Month Headers**: Row 1, columns E through P (January 2016 through December 2016)
- **Answer Position**: E2:P2 (labeled "Current")

## Python/openpyxl Approach

The solution read employee data from columns A and B (rows 2-22) in Sheet1. For each month header in E1:P1, the logic calculated headcount by:

1. Reading the month date from row 1 (columns E through P)
2. For each employee row (2-22):
   - Checking if Hire Date (column A) was on or before the target month
   - Checking if Term Date (column B) was either empty OR after the target month
   - Counting the employee only if both conditions were true

The corrected formula logic used: `(Hire Date <= Month) AND (Term Date is blank OR Term Date > Month)`

Results were written to cells E2:P2, replacing the existing "Current" row values. This approach properly excluded terminated employees from headcount after their termination month, fixing the original formula's flaw of continuing to count all employees regardless of termination status.
