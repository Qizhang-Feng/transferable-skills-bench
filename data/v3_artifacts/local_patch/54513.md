# Solution Note: Calculating Discounted Price in Excel

The task required calculating the price after applying a 55% discount to an original price of $34.99, with the result to be placed in cell F8 of Sheet1.

The spreadsheet structure showed that Sheet1 contained discount-related data in rows 3-5. Row 3 contained headers including 'Quantity', 'Discount', and 'Wholesale' in columns B, C, and D respectively. The discount values were stored in column C, with 0.5 in C4 and 0.55 in C5, representing 50% and 55% discounts.

The Python/openpyxl approach involved:
1. Loading the workbook and accessing Sheet1
2. Identifying that the target cell was F8 (column F, row 8)
3. Applying the discount formula: original_price × (1 - discount_rate)
4. For a 55% discount (0.55), the calculation was: 34.99 × (1 - 0.55) = 34.99 × 0.45
5. Writing the computed result directly to cell F8

The solution required no reading from existing data columns since the original price ($34.99) and discount rate (55% or 0.55) were provided in the task instructions rather than being present in the spreadsheet. The calculation was performed programmatically and the final discounted price was written to the specified answer position F8 in Sheet1.
