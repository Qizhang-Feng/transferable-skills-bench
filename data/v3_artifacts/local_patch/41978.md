# Solution Note: Counting Open Cases by Year

The task required counting open cases per year in the **Cumulative** sheet. The data structure had:
- **Column G** (Year): containing year values starting from row 14 through row 185
- **Column J** (Resolution): containing status values ("open", "closed", etc.) for the same row range (J14:J185)
- **Column I** (rows 2-11): the target location for formulas that count open cases by year

The header row was in row 1, with summary data in rows 2-11 showing years in column G (G2:G11) and requiring open case counts in column I (I2:I11).

The Python/openpyxl approach involved:
1. Reading the year values from **column G, rows 2-11** to identify which years needed counting
2. For each year in the summary section, creating a COUNTIFS formula that:
   - Matched the year from the data range **G14:G185** against the specific year in column G of that row
   - Checked if the corresponding cell in **J14:J185** contained "open" (case-insensitive)
3. Writing the formula to **column I, rows 2-11** (I2:I11)
4. Preserving all original cell formatting in column I, including fonts, borders, fills, and alignment

The formula structure used COUNTIFS with two criteria: year matching and status equaling "open", ensuring accurate counts for each year's open cases.
